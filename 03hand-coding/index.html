Curl Types

Knowing your curl type matters more than you think. Understanding your curl pattern is essential for selecting appropriate hair care products and styling techniques. It helps you tailor your hair routine to maximize the health and beauty of your unique curls. Whether your goal is to define your waves, enhance your curls, or maintain your coils, identifying your curl type is the first step towards achieving your desired look and embracing your natural texture.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair falls between straight and curly. It typically forms an 'S' shape with loose, gentle waves. Type 2 hair can range from barely noticeable waves (2A) to more defined, beachy waves (2B) to pronounced 'S' patterns (2C).

Curly Hair

Type 3 hair features well-defined curls that range from loose spirals to tight corkscrews. It often has a springy, bouncy texture. Type 3 includes 3A (big, loose curls), 3B (small, well-defined ringlets), and 3C (tight corkscrew curls).

Coily Hair

Coily or kinky hair has a dense, tightly packed curl pattern. These curls are characterized by their 'z'-shaped or 's'-shaped coils. Type 4 hair is divided into 4A (tight, well-defined coils), 4B (zigzag or 'z'-shaped patterns), and 4C (small, densely packed coils with minimal curl definition).
